Frequently Asked Questions

What is the lifespan of a solar panel system? ▼

Most solar panel systems are designed to last 25–30 years with minimal degradation in performance. High-quality panels like those we install typically maintain over 80% of their original output after 25 years.

Do I still pay an electric bill with solar? ▼

It depends on your system size and local utility policies. While you may still have a small connection fee, many customers eliminate their usage charges completely. With net metering, excess energy you generate can even earn credits.

What happens during a power outage? ▼

Standard grid-tied solar systems will shut down for safety during outages. However, if you have battery storage, you can continue to power essential loads. This is why we recommend our hybrid systems with backup batteries.

How much can I save with solar? ▼

Savings vary based on your location, energy consumption, and system size. On average, homeowners save significantly over 25 years. Commercial installations see even greater returns due to higher energy usage and tax incentives.

Do solar panels work in cloudy weather? ▼

Yes! Solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy days, though at reduced efficiency. Modern panels are designed to capture diffuse light. Kenya's equatorial climate means excellent solar irradiance even on overcast days.
